Notable Mars Rover Missions

  • Spirit & Opportunity (2004) The twin rovers Spirit and Opportunity were launched in 2003 and landed on Mars in 2004. Originally planned for a 90-day mission, both far exceeded expectations. Opportunity operated for nearly 15 years, uncovering strong evidence that Mars once had liquid water.
  • Curiosity (2012 – Present) The Curiosity rover, part of NASA’s Mars Science Laboratory mission, landed in Gale Crater in 2012. It continues to study Mars’ climate and geology, and has found evidence that ancient Mars could have supported microbial life.
  • Perseverance (2021 – Present) The Perseverance rover landed in Jezero Crater in 2021. It is searching for signs of ancient life and collecting rock samples that may eventually be returned to Earth. It also carries the Ingenuity helicopter, the first aircraft to fly on another planet.
